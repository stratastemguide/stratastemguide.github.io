Forms of Energy

Back to Physics Back to Path

Think of energy as the ability to do work or cause change. It’s never created or destroyed; it just keeps switching costumes. This fundamental principle governs everything from the movement of planets to the vibration of atoms.

1. Mechanical Energy

Mechanical energy is the sum of an object's motion and its position. It is categorized into two main types:

A. Kinetic Energy

The energy of motion. Any moving object possesses this energy. Think of a 10-ton truck moving at 60 mph. That motion is a massive "bank" of energy. It’s highly organized; every atom in that truck is heading in the same direction.

$KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$

B. Potential Energy

This is energy stored due to an object's position or state. There are two primary forms:

  • Potential (Gravitational): Energy in a body because of its height. A boulder on a mountain has "high-quality" potential energy because it’s easy for it to fall and release energy into other forms like kinetic or sound.
  • Potential (Elastic): Energy stored in a stressed solid. When you stretch a rubber band, you force atoms into an uncomfortable alignment. They want to snap back to their low-energy state.

The willingness of matter to return to a stable configuration causes Elastic Potential Energy.

2. Chemical Energy

This is Low Entropy energy at its finest. In a gallon of gasoline or a piece of chocolate, energy is packed into tight, stable molecular bonds.

The "Compact" Factor

Chemical energy is highly ordered. Because the energy is "locked" in a small space, we can transport it easily and release it exactly when we need it.

3. Electrical Energy

Electrons are tiny, but when they move in sync, they carry massive amounts of work-potential.

Utility: Electricity is the most "useful" form because it’s low entropy and easy to convert into light, heat, or motion with very little waste.

4. Nuclear Energy

This is the most "compact" energy in the universe. Instead of rearranging molecules (Chemical), we are splitting or fusing the nucleus of the atom itself.

The energy density here is millions of times higher than chemical energy. Small amounts of nuclear fuel like Uranium and Plutonium can release tremendous energy.

5. Radiant (Light) & Sound Energy

Radiant Energy

Photons (light particles) carry energy as Radiation. It travels through the vacuum of space at the speed of light.

Sound Energy

A "mechanical wave" that travels through a medium. It’s less "useful" for doing work because it spreads out and dissipates into the environment very quickly.

Entropy: Why Heat is "Trash" Energy

In physics, we rank energy by how ordered it is. Entropy is the measure of disorderliness in a system.

High-Quality (Low Entropy)

Energy that is organized and concentrated. It can be easily converted to other forms to do work.

Examples: Electricity, a spinning flywheel, or a tank of hydrogen.

Sort of like your room after your parents pressure you to clean it—everything has its place!

Low-Quality (High Entropy)

Energy that is highly disordered and dispersed. It is difficult to use effectively to do work.

Example: Waste heat. Unless there is a clear boundary of entropic difference (temperature gradient), this energy is "lost" to the system.

Inevitably, both your room and any physical system will become more disordered to reach a state of high stability.
